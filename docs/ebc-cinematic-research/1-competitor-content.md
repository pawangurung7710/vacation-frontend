### What Travelers Actually Experience
- Flight to Lukla (2,860m): thrilling mountain airstrip.
- Namche Bazaar (3,440m): Sherpa capital and acclimatization hub.
- Tengboche Monastery (3,867m): the spiritual heart of the Khumbu.
- Dingboche (4,410m): acclimatization stop in an alpine basin.
- Thukla Memorials: moving monuments to climbers who never returned.
- Everest Base Camp (5,364m): the world's most famous expedition starting ground.
- Kala Patthar (5,545m): best close-up viewpoint of Everest.

### The Difficulty & Experience
- Described as moderate to challenging, but not technical. The real challenge is altitude, cumulative fatigue, cold mornings.
- "You walk, you rest, you eat, you adapt, and the noise of modern routine starts to fall away."
- 5-7 hours walking most days.

## 2. Discovery World Trekking (Logistical & Data Focus)
**Source:** https://www.discoveryworldtrekking.com/trips/everest-base-camp-trek

### Highlights & Facts
- Reaching Kala Patthar (5,555m) for the best sunrise/sunset over Everest.
- Panoramic views of Lhotse (8,516m), Cho Oyu (8,201m), and Makalu (8,463m).
- 40-minute flight from Kathmandu to Lukla (2,860m) or a drive to Manthali (Ramechhap) and a 20-minute flight during peak season.
- 13 nights total: 6 nights in lower-altitude villages (rooms with attached bathrooms), 7 nights in higher-altitude villages (standard rooms with shared facilities).

### Safety & Health Protocols
- **The "Meat Rule":** Do not consume a non-vegetarian diet (mainly meat) during the trek. Meat is often carried up the mountain by porters or yaks without refrigeration, increasing the risk of food poisoning.
- **Gut Health:** Prioritize Gut Health by selecting teahouses with strong hygiene standards. Focus on Dal Bhat, vegetable curries, and soups. No alcohol and tobacco as they slow acclimatization and can mask HACE symptoms.
- **Emergency Helicopter Evacuation:** Standby 24/7 coordination team for severe AMS, HAPE, or HACE.
- **Medical Kits:** Equipped with oximeters to monitor blood oxygen saturation levels.

### Packing & Gear
- Strict Lukla flight baggage limit: 10-15 kg per person (5 kg hand carry + 10 kg luggage/duffel).
- Porter weight limit: 18 kg max (shared between 2 trekkers, so 9 kg per trekker).
- Essential gear listed comprehensively (head, upper body, hands, lower body, footwear, toiletries, accessories).

### Day-to-Day Itinerary (Overview)
- Day 1: Flight to Lukla, trek to Phakding.
- Day 2: Phakding to Namche Bazaar.
- Day 3: Acclimatization at Namche.
- Day 4: Namche to Tengboche.
- Day 5: Tengboche to Dingboche.
- Day 6: Acclimatization at Dingboche.
- Day 7: Dingboche to Lobuche.
- Day 8: Lobuche to EBC to Gorak Shep.
- Day 9: Kala Patthar hike, descend to Pheriche.
- Day 10-14: Return via Tengboche, Namche, Phakding to Lukla, and fly back to Kathmandu.
